About Madiun

Madiun is a city in western East Java, renowned as a center of trade, education, and industry. The city is often nicknamed "Kota Gadis" and is also widely known as "Kota Pecel" due to its famous pecel rice specialty. Moreover, Madiun serves as a strategic transportation route connecting Surabaya with Yogyakarta.

Not only renowned for its culinary delights and strategic location, Madiun also boasts a strong cultural identity. One of the most well-known is pencak silat, a traditional martial art integral to local life, making Madiun a hub for pencak silat schools in Indonesia.

Madiun Regency surrounds the city and encompasses a larger rural area, dominated by fertile lands at the foot of Mount Wilis. The peaceful and green atmosphere of the regency contrasts with the city's modern urban life.

The regency is also rich in natural and cultural tourism, including destinations such as the Grape Tourism Forest, Seweru Waterfall, and Kresek Monument. Traditional events like the Village Clean-Up are still preserved as part of the community's culture.

Thus, Madiun City and Madiun Regency complement each other: the city serves as a center of modernization and commerce, while the regency preserves the culture, traditions, and natural beauty of the area.
